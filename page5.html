Useful Info

MEDICAL SERVICES
The hotel can arrange for a doctor to come to your room if needed. Also, you can see the doctor directly in a mini-clinic of certain Hotels or at the polyclinic located in the village. Doctors are available 24 hours a day and the cost vary depending on the time of the day and care received.
It is recommended to buy a medical insurance before you leave.
In Cuba no vaccine is mandatory but the Twinrix (Hepatitis A + B) is advised.
The clinic in the village is for minor injuries and basic treatments so in the case of serious injuries you will be immediately transported to Havana. There is also a dental clinic.

BEAUTY SALON
There is a beauty salon in the major Hotels and they are often located in the same building of gym and spa facilities:
African braids: 25 CUC
Cut: 8 CUC
Shampoo: 6-8 CUC
Styling: 6-8 CUC
Manicure: 6 CUC

MASSAGE:
Most hotels in Cayo Largo offer professional massage whether in private room or in a hut near the pool or beach.

INTERNET
You can access the internet at the following hotels:
Sol Cayo Largo: 2 stations
Sol Pelicano: 3 stations
Ole Playa Blanca: 2 stations
Isla del Sur: 1 station
It costs 10 CUC per hour and the connection is quite slow.

PHONES
Here are the communication fees at Cuba:
Local: 0.10 @ 0.60 CUC/minute
National long distance calls: 0.70 @ 2.40 CUC/minute
International long distance calls:
Canada, USA and Mexico: 2 @ 3.25 CUC/minute
Central and South America: 4.50 @ 5.90 CUC/minute
Others: 7.80 @ 8 CUC/minute

ROADS
There is only one paved road which connects the hotels to the airport and the village. The driving is on the right side. There are a few other roads but they are small sandy trails, so take care if you rent a scooter. Driving under the influence of alcohol is a very serious offence in Cuba so be very careful.




WATER
You can drink water in the hotels, however, is doesn’t taste good. You can buy a bottle of water and ask for a (free) refill of purified water at the bar in the hotels.

MOSQUITOS
Mosquitos tend to be a popular complaint between tourists in Cayo Largo especially during summer. They usually appear only at sunset, but it depends on the climatic conditions (mainly humidity). Hotels god the surrounding every night as a preventive measure; however, it is recommended that you bring a good insect spray repellent.

MONEY
There are two currencies available in Cuba, CUPs which are generally used by the Cubans and CUCs that are used by both, foreign and Cubans. All cash purchases in Cuba are made in CUC or CUP, no other foreign currency is accepted in the island. Tipping is expected and made in CUC.
You get the best exchange rate in banks; however, the most convenient way is to exchange your money, for a little bit higher rate, in a Cadeca which is the official government’s currency exchange house. They can be found everywhere including airports and most hotels and resorts. You can also exchange it in most hotel receptions but the rate is usually a lot higher. Your passport is required to exchange money at a bank or Cadeca but not at the hotels. It is important to note that USD exchange rate against CUC is different, a 10% tax is added to avoid bringing US dollars to the island.
The Banco Nacional de Cuba publishes the official daily exchange rates in its website: www.bc.gob.cu

Cards:
MasterCard and Visa cards (from a non-US bank) are accepted in most hotels, resorts, restaurants, stores and tours agencies. They can also be used in banks or Cadecas to withdraw money. Only Visa or MasterCard Debit cards work in Cuba but in an ATM only the Visa one.

Language: Spanish
Required documents: Valid passport + Visa Tourist Card
